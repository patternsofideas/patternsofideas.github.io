This is the final post of the series on percolation. It concludes the proof of Harris-Kesten by using a technique called dependent percolation to conclude that pT≥12p_T\geq \frac{1}{2}. In conjunction with the result of the previous post, that pH=12p_H=\frac{1}{2}, and the obvious fact that pT≤pHp_T\leq p_H, this allows us to conclude that pT=12p_T=\frac{1}{2} as well.

1. Dependent Percolation

Let GG be a graph and ℙ̃\tilde{\mathbb{P} } a probability measure on the set of site configurations of GG. We call this measure kk-independent if |S−T|=mins∈S,t∈T|s−t|≥k|S-T|=\min_{s\in S, t\in T} |s-t| \geq k means that the states of SS are independent of the states of GG. (The distance used here is the one induced by GG.) The k=1k=1 case of this is just independent percolation, but k>1k>1 is a bit more interesting.

The result we’ll need is the following lemma:

Lemma 1. Suppose k,Δ≥2k, \Delta\geq 2 are positive integers and GG is a graph with maximum degree Δ\Delta. There exist constants p1p_1 and aa, both depending on kk and Δ\Delta, such that if ℙ̃\tilde{\mathbb{P} } is a kk-independent percolation measure in which each site is open with probability at most p1p_1, ℙ̃(|Cv|≥n)≤e−an\tilde{\mathbb{P} }(|C_v|\geq n)\leq e^{-an} for all v∈V(G)v\in V(G).

Proof. Suppose |Cv|≥n|C_v|\geq n. Then the open subgraph contains a tree with nn vertices, include vv. There are at most (eΔ)n−1(e\Delta)^{n-1} such trees, so fix one. If ww is some other vertex of GG, at most 1+Δ+⋯+Δk−1≤Δk1+\Delta+\cdots+\Delta^{k-1}\leq \Delta^k vertices of GG are within k−1k-1 units of ww. So there is a subset SS of size at least nΔ−kn\Delta^{-k} of the vertices of TT so that any a,b∈Sa,b\in S are at least kk apart. (This can be constructed greedily.)

These vertices in SS are open independently, so the probability of every vertex of TT being open is at most p1|S|p_1^{|S|}. 2. Removing Dependence

The last step in our proof is to make the leap from the result about dependent percolation to an analogous result about standard bond percolation.

Theorem 2. For bond percolation on ℤ2\mathbb{Z} ^2, if p<12p<\frac{1}{2}, there is a constant aa such that ℙp(|C0|≥n)≤e−an.\mathbb{P} _p(|C_0|\geq n)\leq e^{-an}.
